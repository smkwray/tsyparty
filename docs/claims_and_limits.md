# Claims and limits

## Allowed claims

- Banks' Treasury additions were most likely funded net by sectors A, B, C.
- Foreign Treasury additions were most likely funded net by sectors A, B, C.
- Primary-market buyer composition shifted toward investor class X.
- Sector Y behaves more like banks than sector Z does.
- Dealer warehousing likely bridged part of a transition episode.

## Disallowed claims

- Sector A directly sold to sector B in exact amount X.
- Dealers were the immediate counterparty on every inferred path.
- Foreign beneficial owner identity is recovered exactly.
- Household-sector residual movements are a literal household trade flow.

## Main limits

1. FWTW is a levels framework, not a complete public transactions framework.
2. TIC is residence-based and can reflect custodial location.
3. Household buckets in financial accounts can be partly residual.
4. Net flows do not reveal gross churn.
5. Weekly and monthly event layers should not be overread as full ownership traces.

## Communication rule

Every chart, table, or paragraph that uses the inferred matrix should say **likely net counterparties** or equivalent.
